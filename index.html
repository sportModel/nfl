Predictions are available for the 2016 NFL season, as well as parameter estimates for each team's points scored and allowed, adjusting for opponent. The predictions are generated by a Bayesian model that assumes a t-distribution for the two scores in a football game. The t-distribution (25 df), which has slightly wider tails than a Gaussian, is more robust to outliers (i.e., blowouts). The degrees of freedom were chosen empirically in accordance with data from past years.

Predictions are given for all weeks of the season based on the available data at that time. The most recent predictions for a given week are kept posted so that they may be looked back on and judged. For example, the predictions for Week 6 keep changing every week until we obtain Week 5 data, after which it remains as the prediction for Week 6 given data from Weeks 1-5 (and prior seasons).
